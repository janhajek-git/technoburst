The Intel Core i5-11600K is one of Intel’s most solid desktop processors. It can handle most of the daily home and office computing tasks flawlessly. It also features a thermal design power of 125W.

However, it does not have the required thermal solution, and the maximum allowed temperature is 212 degrees Fahrenheit. For a processor as powerful as this one, you may need a CPU cooler. 

To make its performance sustainable, you may have to invest in the best CPU cooler for i5. This way, you can ensure you get the maximum efficiency from your PC.

In this article, we shall look at some of the best CPU coolers for i5-11600K available in the market. Let’s dive into it!

Quick Tips for Shopping Cooler for Your i5 11600k 

Before we indulge in the choices, let’s understand how to pick the best CPU cooler for i5. Here are some important factors you must look for when shopping for this product.

Thermal Design Power

Thermal Design Power or TDP is a vital factor to consider before purchasing a CPU cooler. TDP refers to the amount of heat a CPU produces when it works at its base clock. The TDP of this particular processor is 125 W. 

Hence, check the thermal design power of the cooler before purchasing it. In many cases, the air and liquid coolers specify the rating in their listing. However, if they do not, you can look up about them online.

If you buy a cooler with a lower TDP rating, the fan will function loudly, and the CPU may throttle. It can be damaging to the system in the longer run. So, it is wise to invest in a cooler with higher TDP ratings that run soundlessly and lower your CPU’s temperature significantly.

Types of Coolers

Primarily there are two types of coolers that you can choose from for your CPU. They are air coolers and water coolers.

Air Cooler

Air coolers can be an amazing choice for you if you have a custom desktop or laptop. Moreover, air coolers are the most popular kind of additional coolers when you need more than a stock cooler.

Generally, air coolers are larger than water coolers. Hence, it can not fit perfectly with multiple PC cases. Further, most air coolers, especially aftermarket ones, run at higher speeds and make louder noises.

Water Cooler

Water coolers or liquid coolers are an upgrade to air coolers. Usually, they perform better than air coolers at maintaining the temperature of the processor. Plus, they are available in a wide range of sizes to fit almost any system size.

However, you can avoid this if you do not use your system frequently and do not overheat all that much. Moreover, water coolers can be more expensive and potentially hazardous if the liquid ever leaks. 

Integrated Heat Spreader

Integrated Heat Spreader or IHS is the name for the top silver cover part on a CPU. It is a flat copper plate that protects the core of the CPU. So, why does IHS matter?

It is important because IHS provides a pathway for heat exchange between the cooler and the CPU. It also protects the processing silicon. Usually, soldered IHS is better than unsoldered since it provides a better path for heat exchange.

Thermal Interface Material

Thermal Interface Material or TIM is a very smooth component that bridges the IHS and the cooler. The smoother it is, the better the conductibility of the unit. However, it is very difficult to manufacture a completely smooth TIM. Hence, they use conductive or non-conductive pastes to smoothen it artificially. 

Quiet Operation

Most people prefer a quiet operating system over a noisy one. It is especially true if you are a gamer, graphic designer, or content creator. You would prefer to work in an environment with fewer noisy disturbances.

Therefore, it is a good factor to look for when buying a new cooler for your PC. Moreover, most high-quality coolers ensure to incorporate quiet operation as a feature.

Style

CPU coolers usually stand out in the crowd of numerous components. Hence, it is safe to say the style of the coolers is an important factor to consider. So, do not forget to choose a cooler based on its style after its performance.

Moreover, there are many types of coolers available in the market. They come in a multitude of sizes, shapes, colors, and designs. So, pick what you like so the aesthetic will be pleasing every time you see through the case window.

RGB

Many people prefer a color with an RGB customization option. If you are one of them, you should definitely go for it! You can choose from a wide variety of RGB coolers that offer RGB lighting and fans.

Additionally, it is important to ensure you can turn on and off the RGB whenever you want to. It can have a built-in controller or a compatible motherboard header while you plug it in. 

Nonetheless, it can be helpful when you can turn off the RGB lights without turning off your system.

How Do I Know What Cooler Will Fit in My System?

No matter which type or style of cooler you are opting for, you need to ensure it fits your system. So, how can you know if the cooler you like fits your CPU?

Firstly, you must check for the CPU socket and any potential chassis limitations for cooler height or radiator size for i5 11600k. A socket is a part that provides an electrical and mechanical connection between the motherboard and the processor.

You can check the “Socket Supported” in the CPU package, its official website, or the product listing. Then, compare the socket information, the TPD of the i5 11600k, with the socket info on the cooler. 

If it matches, you know the cooler is going to fit in your system. Moreover, the socket supported for i5-11600k is FCLGA1200.

Installing CPU Cooler for i5-11600k

To ensure you are installing your CPU cooler correctly, I am providing you a guide. Here is a step-by-step process of installing a CPU cooler.

Step #1. Apply Thermal Paste

Before you fix your cooler in your CPU, check for thermal paste. It is a thin layer of a thermal compound that removes the gap between the cooler’s base and the CPU lid. Most coolers come with thermal paste in place, yet not all of them do.

So, if it is not there, you can apply the thermal paste. Remember to apply only a small amount. All you have to do is apply a small dot of the paste in the center of the CPU lid.

Step #2. Set Up Standoffs

This step is an optional step depending on the cooler and motherboard combination. You can use a standoff that screws through the motherboard into the case or a bracket that sits behind the motherboard. 

If the cooler you purchase comes with these components, attach them after seeing if they are appropriate. Also, if you are using a water cooler, you have to install the radiator in your case.

Step #3. Attach the Cooler

Firstly, line up the screws with the holes in the motherboard carefully. Then, lower the cooler into the CPU and screw in the connectors. You can use an X pattern to screw it tight.

Start by tightening loosely at first, then increase the tension as you go on. Make sure to screw in evenly across the board since it can create uneven pressure distribution if you do not. Then, attach any fans or connect them to the radiator depending on the type of cooler.

Step #4. Plug-In

Once you attach everything in the CPY, you have to plug in everything. All parts need power despite the type of cooler. The plugging varies from one cooler to another so that you can connect it according to its design.
