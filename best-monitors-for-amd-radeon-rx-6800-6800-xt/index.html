The AMD Radeon RX 6800 and Radeon RX 6800 XT are upon us, and gaming PC enthusiasts are excited to get their hands on these top-of-the-line graphics cards, that display an amazing performance to value ratio.

Not only can these GPUs break the 120 FPS mark at 1440p, while costing only $579 and $649 respectively, but they also consistently reach 60 FPS at 4K; as shown in the 10 gaming titles that AMD recently tested. In these benchmarks, AMD proves that their GPUs display the best FPS per dollar expended. In fact, out of 20 comparisons carried out, the Radeon RX 6800 and Radeon RX 6800 XT come out on top of all their competition in 19; only trailing the GeForce RTX 3080 for The Division 2 at 4K.

To output this astounding graphical processing potential, you will need a monitor of the same caliber. To assist you in finding the right one for your needs, we have compiled a list of the best monitors to pair with the AMD Radeon RX 6800 and 6800 XT – with price and value always in mind.

Best Budget 1080P 144 Hz Monitor for Radeon RX 6800 & 6800 XT – Viewsonic Elite XG2405

Combining a Zen 3 Ryzen 5000 Series CPU with a Radeon RX 6800 or RX 6800 XT is definitely not cheap, so it is totally understandable if you are looking for a monitor that won’t break the bank. Luckily, Viewsonic offers an excellent choice that offers quality visuals and performance at an under $200 price.

The Elite XG2405 is a 24-inch monitor with an IPS panel that has a bit-depth of 8 bits; utilizing 6 bits together with Frame Rate Control (FRC). The screen can display 1.68 million colors, covering 104% of the sRGB spectrum and 72% of the NTSC color space. It has a slightly low brightness level of 250 nits, a static contrast of 1,000 to one, a viewing angle (both vertical and horizontal) of 178 degrees, and a Grey to Grey (G2G) response time of only 1 millisecond.

The Elite XG2405 features a full range of possible ergonomic flexibility, allowing for height adjustment (120 mm), a landscape/portrait pivot, a right pivot (90 degrees), a left/right swivel (90/90 degrees), and a forward/back tilt (5/20 degrees). Additionally, two 2-Watt speakers, two HDMI 1.4 slots, one DP1.2 port, and one audio-out jack are included.

The best part of the Viewsonic Elite XG2405? It costs only $179.99.

Best 1080P 240 Hz Monitor for Radeon RX 6800 & 6800 XT – Alienware 25 AW2518HF

Next, we have an option for competitive gaming enthusiasts. One that combines low input lag with extremely smooth framerates: The Alienware 25 AW2518HF.

This monitor features a TN panel – meaning incredible responsiveness at the cost of the viewing angle. The panel has a bit depth of 8 bits (6 + FRC) with a large display area of 90.29%. Its W-LED backlight can display 1.68 million colors, and the screen can reach an impressive brightness level of 400 nits. Finally, the G2G response time of this monitor stands at 1 millisecond, while the total input lag can reach as low as 19 milliseconds.

The ergonomic versatility of this monitor is identical to the aforementioned Viewsonic Elite (with only smaller swivel and tilting angles), though, where this monitor shines, is its connectivity. It has a total of five USB 3.0 slots (one upstream, three downstream, one 5V/1.5A), as well as two HDMI 2.0 slots, one DisplayPort 1.2 slot, and two audio jacks (in and out).

The Alienware 25 AW2518HF can be purchased for $349.99 – not cheap, but definitely worth the cost for its premium-level performance.

Best 1440P 144 Hz Monitor for Radeon RX 6800 & 6800 XT – ASUS TUF Gaming VG27AQ

The perfect middle-ground between competitive gaming and AAA titles can be found in the 1440p 144 Hz resolution/refresh rate. The best monitor by far in this category is ASUS’s TUF Gaming VG27AQ.

Not only does this monitor exceed the standard 144 Hz refresh rate, having the ability to overclock to 165 Hz, but it also features a 1,200:1 static contrast ratio, an input lag of only 5.3 ms (Motion Picture Response Time (MPRT) of 1 ms, G2G is 4 ms) and a peak brightness level of 350 nits. The W-LED backlight can display 1.68 million colors (99% of sRGB spectrum), while the AHVA IPS panel has a bit depth of 8 bits.

A full array of ergonomic features can also be found with the TUF Gaming VG27AQ, similar to the Alienware 25 and the Viewsonic elite. Two 2W speakers are also included, together with two HDMI 2.0 ports, one DP 1.2 slot, and one audio jack. AMD FreeSync, a 5-way OSD joystick, Extreme Low Motion Blue (ELMB), and Shadow boost are but a few of the additional features that can be found in this ASUS monitor.

 Down from its original $549 MSRP, the TUF Gaming VG27AQ can currently be purchased for $419.99.

Best 4K 60 Hz Monitor for Radeon RX 6800 & 6800 XT – ASUS VP28UQG

Another solid option for enjoying the full power that the Radeon RX 6800 and Radeon RX 6800 XT can muster, is a monitor with a 4K resolution at 60 Hz. Best for those looking to enjoy triple-A titles, the ASUS VP27UQG offers amazing performance at an exceptionally low cost.

It has a 28-inch screen with a brightness level of 300 nits, a TN panel that has a bit depth of 10 bits, and a W-LED backlight can display well over one billion colors (100% sRGB). Due to its TN panel, the ASUS VP27UQG has incredibly low input lag. It has a Motion Picture Response Time (MPRT) of only 1 millisecond and an end-to-end input lag as low as 27.4 ms. For those that love challenging, timing-dependent, single players titles, this monitor is a perfect choice.

The ergonomics here are not as robust as the aforementioned options, as this monitor lacks height adjustment, swivel, and landscape/portrait pivot. It does include two HDMI 2.0 ports, one DP 1.2 slot, and one audio-out jack – as well as all the AMD and ASUS exclusive features found in the ASUS TUF Gaming VG27AQ (including SPENDID Video Intelligence Technology and Trace Free Technology).

To make up for its lack of its ergonomics, ASUS VP27UQG has an incredible price, as it can currently be found for only $313.99.
