Curved gaming monitors are the new deal since they have much more to offer than flat monitors. They are not only attractive to look at, but they also provide you with many physical benefits.

Curved monitors offer you a good field of view, so you do not exert your eyes. Just like flat monitors, they come in different sizes. However, unlike flat monitors larger size does not mean more strain on your eyes or increased discomfort.

If you are looking for new gaming monitors, then these 144Hz gaming monitors are the best. They have a standard refresh rate along with enhancing your gaming experience in many different ways.

How To Choose The Best 144Hz Curved Gaming Monitor

Gamers will need to find the right monitor that will enhance their gaming experience. When making this choice, there are several factors that you need to look into. 

Below are some of those factors or features you should consider while buying the best 144Hz curved gaming monitor.

Pixel Density

When it comes to gaming monitors, you should always go for larger screen sizes. The larger your screen size, the better you experience. However, when you increase the size of the screen, you need to keep a note of the pixel density.

Pixel density is the ratio of pixels per inch of the monitor’s size and resolution.

If the pixel density is low, then the image will become pixelated. This means you will be unable to view a clear image, and this will hinder your gaming. Higher pixel density means clearer details and more screen space.

Screen Size

Like I discussed, large screen sizes are not always for the best. It is also because large screen sizes will increase your budget. For most gamers, a 27 inches screen would be best for all types of gaming experiences.

They are large but not too much to create discomfort while playing. If the screen is too large, then you need to move your head from one corner to another too much.

When you choose a 27 inches monitor, the ideal resolution will be 2560 x 1440. In this, the pixel density will be approximately 109 pixels per inch. This ensures that you get the right details and screen space without the requirement of any scaling.

Widescreen / Ultrawide Screen

The aspect ratio for both these screens is different. In Ultrawide screen, (https://www.forbes.com/sites/forbes-personal-shopper/2020/10/28/best-ultrawide-monitors-2020-samsung-aw3420dw/) you get an aspect ratio of 21:9, while in widescreen, you get an aspect ratio of 16:9.

The horizontal space for the Ultrawide screen is 33% more than the widescreen. This makes the gaming experience much more engaging. This is extremely important in certain games where you need the field of view to be wide.

Even though most new games support the Ultrawide ratio, not all would be compatible. Games that are not compatible with the 21:9 ratio will give you black borders on both sides. If you do not wish to keep the black borders, you will have to see a stretched-out image.

Response Time

The response time of your curved gaming monitor is essential. It is the time taken by the monitor to respond to your inputs. When you choose your gaming monitor, ensure that the response time is on the lower scale.

The ideal response time would fall between 1ms and 5 ms. For highly competitive games, it is always advisable to choose a lower response time, preferably 1ms. However, if you are using very large displays, then 5ms would also be quite good.

Panel Types

There are three-panel types that are VA (Vertical Alignment), TN (Twisted Nematic), and IPS (In-Panel Switching).

In TN panels, the color reproduction is not as good as IPS panels, but the refresh rates are high. You will not be satisfied with the viewing angles of TN panels, but this may not be a problem for many.

IPS panels have good image quality and color reproduction. However, the refresh rates of these panels are quite low. VA panels are good for most gaming monitors and feature the good factors of the other two panels.

Why Are The Advantages Of A Curved Gaming Monitor?

Are you unable to decide if you should go for a 144Hz curved gaming monitor or flat monitor? To clear your confusion, I have highlighted some major advantages of curved monitors.

Immersive

If the image displayed on the monitor does not attract you, you will not get a complete gaming experience. In curved monitors, a technology called ocular perception is used, making you see images like in real life.

Curved monitors take into consideration all three dimensions, which are length, breadth, and height. This gives you an immersive experience which you will not find on flat monitors.

Eradicates Distortion

No one likes a blurry image or distortion in the edges of the image. This is a very common issue in flat monitors and not so much in curved monitors. Generally, when screen size increases, the distortion also increases.

However, in curved monitors, you are unlikely to face this issue.

Comfortable

Since the distortion is low or nil, you will be more comfortable using curved monitors. Your eyes will not experience any strain, and you can play games for long hours. Moreover, even with large screens, a curved monitor will not exceed your field of view.

In flat monitors, you will need to move your head to take in the full screen. This becomes a nuisance after a certain time, which is not a problem in curved gaming monitors.

Large Field of View

Like I mentioned in the earlier point, the field of view in curved gaming monitors is better than flat monitors. Both ends of the monitor are curved to ensure that the whole screen stays within your eye range.

You will not have to move your head or eyesight sideways to capture the whole screen. A curved screen ensures that light from all directions is directed to your eye. Hence you do not have to face much ocular exertion.
