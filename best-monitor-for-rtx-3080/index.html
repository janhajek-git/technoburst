The first GPU to launch for Nvidia’s RTX GeForce Ampere 30 Series was the RTX 3080 – and it was a rocky start, to say the least. Within only eight seconds every single RTX 3080 had been sold out, with many of the initial orders only now starting to be fulfilled – a month after launch.

But it wasn’t just Nvidia’s underproduction of the RTX 3080 that made it impossible to find. The specifications of the graphics card, especially for its price, make it ideal for gaming applications at framerates and resolutions that weren’t possible just a few months back. Benchmarks from Gamer’s Nexus show us that the 8,704 CUDA Cores, 30 TFLOPs of FP32 compute performance, GDDR6X RAM, and 2nd & 3rd generation Ray Tracing and Tensor cores respectively are not just good on paper, but can actually perform incredibly well. In comparison to the previous generation RTX 2080 Ti, the RTX 3080 reaches framerates that are 20% to 25% higher, despite being $500 cheaper.

Of course, it must be said that you can have the best gaming PC hardware available, but if you don’t have a monitor that can adequately display the capabilities of the components it is all for naught. The resolutions that the RTX 3080 can support range from 1080p to 4K, while the framerate can vary. For example, if you enjoy competitive online multiplayer titles, then resolution is far less important to the number of frames the monitor can refresh per second. This is due to the fact that monitors with a high framerate will also have an inherently smaller amount of input lag – this effect being further amplified by Nvidia’s new Reflex feature. On the other hand, if you prefer both competitive multiplayers and single player, triple-A titles, then a 1440p resolution at 144 Hz is an excellent choice, as framerates are still high, and the resolution allows for impressive visuals. One step ahead of the 1440p resolution is 4K – which comes in both 60 Hz and 144 Hz variants. Because of how hardware intensive it is to process gaming applications at 4K, the framerates inevitably unstable. Even for the RTX 3080, achieving framerates above 60 Hz will not always be guaranteed. Still, if you are willing to purchase the best motherboard, CPU, and RAM available – and push these components to their limit via overclocking – then 4K at 100 to 120 FPS can still be possible, depending on the game.

To help you find the right outlet to display your RTX 3080’s full potential, we have compiled a list of the four best monitors depending on framerate, resolution, and, of course, price.

Best 1080p Monitor for RTX 3080 – ASUS ROG Swift PG259QN

If you love being competitive in online multiplayer or are actually a professional e-Sports player, a 360 Hz monitor is by far the best choice for an ultra-smooth framerate that provides the least amount of input lag – which is critical for reaction-based games. These monitors have been manufactured in partnership with Nvidia, as they include onboard Nvidia Latency Analyzer hardware that calculates and displays your overall end-to-end input lag (from the moment that a button or click is pressed all the way to actual action in-game) in real-time. With a 360 Hz monitor in unison with the RTX 3080, end-to-end input lag is reduced by anywhere between 15-44 milliseconds.

Of all four announced 360 Hz monitors, ASUS reigns supreme with their PG259QN. This 24.5-inch monitor has a fast-IPS panel that features a 1 millisecond G2G response time, while still displaying 1.68 million colors, a brightness of 400 nits, and a static contrast of 1,000 to 1. In addition, it utilizes Nvidia’s G-Sync to reduce screen tearing and stuttering, as well as ULMB (Ultra Low Motion Blur).

This ROG Swift monitor also has a plethora of ergonomic features, an RGB-lit ROG logo in the back, two USB 3.0 slots, and a host of ASUS exclusive features like GamePlus and GameVisual.

Though arguably a bit expensive, this $699 monitor is well worth its price, especially considering that it is the least costly of all its competition.

Best 1440p 144 Hz Monitor for RTX 3080 – ASUS TUF Gaming VG27AQ

Powered by the RTX 3080, a 1440p 144 Hz monitor can fully display triple-A games in all their graphical glory, without losing the competitive edge for online multiplayer. With both aspects in mind, we have chosen the ASUS TUF Gaming VG27AQ, which displays a wide color range, HDR capability, as well as and ultra-low response time, and input lag.

To be more specific, this 27-inch monitor and its AHVA IPS panel feature a G2G response time of 1 millisecond and an ultra-low display input lag of only 5.3 milliseconds. With a framerate that can reach 165 Hz, a 350-nit brightness, an impressive 1,200 to one static contrast ratio, and a 99% sRGB color space coverage, this monitor is primed to play any type of game you prefer.

The TUF Gaming VG27AQ has similar ergonomics and features to the aforementioned ROG Swift, as well as two integrated 2W speakers. You can currently find it for $549.99, though its price can vary depending on availability.

Best 4K 60 Hz Monitor for RTX 3080 – BenQ EL2870U

Moving up to 4K 60 Hz, we’ve chosen the BenQ EL2870U. This 28-inch monitor features a TN panel with a 10-bit depth and Frame Rate Control that is capable of displaying over a billion colors with a brightness of 300 nits and a 1,000 to one static contrast ratio. Due to the TN panel, this monitor has a 1ms response time and an incredibly low input lag of only 5ms. This comes at the cost of its viewing angle, so the ergonomics will also be somewhat limited. Still, the monitor is VESA compatible and can be mounted onto a wall as well.

The best aspect of the BenQ EL2870 is its price, since you can currently find this monitor for only $299.99; down from the original $499.99 MSRP. An excellent price for an equally all-around excellent monitor.

Best 4K 144 Hz Monitor for RTX 3080 – Acer Predator X27

If you want to go all out with your monitor purchase, nothing beats the Acer Predator X27. This 27-inch monitor uses Full-Array Local Dimming and Quantum Dot technology for an amazing brightness level that can reach 1,000 nits, a color space that reaches well above 1-billion colors, and a 4 ms G2G response time. Despite its IPS panel, this monitor is meant to be viewed from the front, so its ergonomics are slightly limited.

The Acer Predator X27 also includes two 7-Watt speakers, four USB 3.0 ports, and a host of features. Of course, this monitor is far from cheap, costing anywhere from $1,299 to $1,799, but it’s really worth the cost for the display it has to offer. Certain variants of the X27 can currently be found for $1,192 as well. If you’re worried that the RTX 3080 won’t be able to handle 4K at 144 Hz, rest assured benchmarks show it can handle several titles at well over 100 FPS.
