The GTX 1080 is one of Nvidia’s most powerful graphics cards of all time. The GTX 1080 offers extraordinary graphics performance, which will immerse you in a new level of gaming experience. If you already have this amazing graphics card, we might assume that you don’t have any plan to settle for anything less when it comes to your visual performance, and you are looking for the best monitor possible that you can partner with.

If you are looking for the best monitor for a GTX 1080 graphics card, you’re in the right place. We have listed in this guide the best monitors that you can accompany with your GTX 1080. Things To Consider:

Resolution And Refresh Rate

Picking a monitor always depends on what Resolution/Hz you prefer. If you are a competitive gamer and you want the best performance possible, I would recommend you go with a standard resolution and higher refresh rate, so it will be easy for you to achieve a higher frame rate, which results in smoother gameplay performance. On the other side, if you prefer sharper images, getting a higher resolution monitor with a refresh rate that is equivalent to the FPS you’re getting is the best choice for you, just remember that it will be a bit harder to achieve high frames rate since they have a lot of pixels to process and give more workload to your graphics card especially for some graphically demanding games. Here are the recommended resolution and refresh rate pairings:

  • 1080p at 240Hz
  • 1440p at 144Hz
  • 1440p(ultra wide) at 100Hz
  • 2160p (4k) at 60Hz

G-Sync Compatibility

You need to make sure that the monitor you’ll get is a G-Sync compatible since Nvidia’s GTX 1080 features G-Sync technology, so you will be able to take advantage of it. G-Sync technology primarily aims to resolve screen tearing issues, which result in a satisfying visual experience. Also, for some of you who aren’t aware of this, there are two types of adaptive-sync, G-Sync is for Nvidia and FreeSync is for AMD, so you need to be careful that you only choose G-Sync monitor for your GTX 1080.

Monitor Screen Size

In terms of monitor screen size, it is often a big deal for most consumers when they are deciding which monitor is the perfect one for them. For competitive gamers, they often prefer standard size monitors because it helps them better focus on the game, and a 24-inch size monitor is a sweet spot for them, especially in 1080p gaming. However, if you decided to go with a high-resolution monitor, getting a much larger screen is a better choice so you will enjoy the real beauty of it, and anything smaller will result in redundant resolution. We’ve listed different types of monitors that balances the screen size and resolution, so you don’t have to worry about getting redundant resolution or a pixelated screen.
