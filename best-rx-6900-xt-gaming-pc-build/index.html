CPU – Ryzen 9 5900X Overview:

The AMD Ryzen 9 5900X marks a decades-long battle between Intel and AMD. The two chipmakers have consistently maintained or dominated a competitor in multi-core and single-core performance. Usually, AMD Ryzen 9 5900X contains 12 processor cores. The AMD Ryzen 9 5900X processor is based on 7nm process technology and Zen 3 architecture, whereas its base clock speed is 3.70 GHz, whereas the maximum clock speed mode is 4.80 GHz. 

The Zen 3 family of processors are available in the market, significantly improving the already impressive Zen 2 architecture. One of the best new processors is AMD’s Ryzen 9 5900X, which comes second behind Ryzen 9 5950X. This 12-core, 24-thread processor is named World’s Best Gaming Processor by AMD. The AMD Ryzen 9 5900X is a great processor for both gaming and any other workload.

With the Ryzen 9 5900X alongside other processors in the series, such as the AMD Ryzen 5800X, there is no reason left to buy an Intel processor for your next gaming PC. AMD not only delivers better performance across the board because Intel still does not implement PCIe 4.0 support, but AMD processors are also more feature-rich and more future-oriented.
