There hasn’t been a greater time for PC builders and PC gaming enthusiasts alike. New Nvidia and AMD GPUs on the horizon, new PCIe 4.0 SSDs, we’ve reached the peak in DDR4 memory performance, and now we also have the new and improved Ryzen 5000, Zen3, processors.

It is impressive to see how AMD has been extremely smart in managing their resources; focusing on the aspects that matter most in regards to the improvement of their Ryzen processors. Since Zen2, AMD has always held the upper hand against Intel in regards to the core and thread count of their CPUs, together with their smaller processing node, their PCIe 4.0 compatibility, and their larger L3 caches. This generation around, seeing that their Ryzen 3000 series still lead Intel in this regard, instead of increasing their core and thread count, they instead opted for increasing their clock speeds and, more importantly, reducing their internal latencies. Using the same technology found in the Ryzen 3 3300X, which had a unified CCX (Core Complex) that contained all of its cores and L3 cache within the same CCD (chiplet die) – which resulted in far less core to core latency and allowed this inexpensive 4-core processor to reach incredibly high gaming performance – the Ryzen 5000 Series CPUs now feature double the amount of cores and threads within the same CCX. With 8 cores feeding off one 32 MB L3 cache, latency is reduced immensely, meaning much better gaming performance without increasing either the cost of manufacturing, or the power consumption.

If you’re looking to build a brand-new gaming desktop, then the most important component to pair with the CPU is the motherboard. The motherboard is what feeds the processor the proper amount of power in order to make full use of its overclocking capabilities, as well as keep its temperature low enough so that it doesn’t overheat and impact its lifespan. It is also what will allow you to make use of the PCIe 4.0 capabilities of the AM4 chipset, its RAM compatibility, and much more.

To help you find a suitable motherboard to pair with your Ryzen 9 5900X purchase, we’ve compiled a list of the best motherboards, depending on price, function, and form factor.

So, which are these best motherboards for Ryzen 5000 Series CPUs? Let’s find out.

Best Budget Motherboard for Ryzen 9 5900X – Gigabyte B550 AORUS Elite

The Ryzen 9 5900X does not come cheap, so if you’re looking for hardware components on a budget, this B550 motherboard under $200 is ideal.

We speak, of course, of the Gigabyte AORUS Elite B550. This Motherboard features a 12+2 Phase VRM solution, with 50-amp power stages and the ability to hold a total of 128 GB of DDR4 RAM with a frequency of up to 4,000 MHz. In addition, it has two M.2 slots (one of which is PCIe 4.0 compatible), as well as four SATA III 6 GB/s slots – so, all in all, plenty of space for storage.

The audio codec is the standard for high-quality motherboards, Realtek ALC 1200, and, in terms of extra connectivity, the AORUS Elite offers two USB 3.2 Gen 2 slots and four USB 3.2 Gen 1 slots.

A solid VRM solution, paired with plenty of features, high connectivity, and a solid audio codec, the Gigabyte AORUS Elite is definitely worth its $149.99 price tag.

Best Gaming Motherboard for Ryzen 9 5900X – ASUS ROG Crosshair VIII Hero

Going a step above, and into the high-end gaming territory of the Ryzen 9 5900X, means that we need a motherboard with an equally powerful VRM and cooling solution that can handle these new Ryzen processors. For this task, there is none better than the ASUS ROG Crosshair VIII Hero X570 motherboard.

With its 14+2 phase 60-amp VRM solution, which is thermally controlled by heatsinks with embedded heat pipes, the VIII Hero can handle high amounts of overclocking – both in bursts, as well as for extended periods of time. The motherboard can support 128 GB of 4,600 MHz DDR4 RAM, two M.2 PCIe 4.0 & eight SATA 6 GB/s SSDs, and it also features a high-end Supreme FX S1220 audio codec with an ESS ES9023P converter and a TI RC4580 IO AMP.

Though a bit costly, this $359.99 motherboard is worth every penny for the amount of performance, value, and even style, that it has to offer.

Best Workstation Motherboard for Ryzen 9 5900X – MSI Meg X570 Unify

In order to utilize the full power of the AMD Ryzen 9 5900X, for workstation applications – through high-end gaming is also possible – you won’t find a better motherboard than the MSI MEG X570 Unify.

The MegX570 Unify trades RGB and other gaming fluff for functionality, featuring a 12+2+1 phase VRM design that utilizes 60-amp power stages with 12 titanium chokes. It has large extended heatsinks, as well as a built-in fan, AI-tuning technology, and a server-grade PCB. Memory-wise, it can hold up to 128 GB of DDR4 RAM, with speeds of up to an incredible 5,000 MHz.

It also includes three M.2 sockets, four SATA 6 GB/s slots, and plenty of PCIe 4.0 lane and USB connectivity. Finally, it features a Realtek ALC 1220 audio codec.

Surprisingly enough, you can find the MSI Meg X570 Unify for only $299.99, a must-buy for professional PC setups.

Best ITX Motherboard for Ryzen 9 5900X – Gigabyte X570I AORUS Pro Wi-Fi

Last but not least, we have an ITX motherboard for those looking to make an SFF build using the Ryzen 9 5900X CPU: The Gigabyte X570I AORUS Pro Wi-Fi.

This motherboard may be small, but it sure is powerful. It has an all-true (no doublers or parallels) 8-phase VRM design with 40A PowlRstage power stages and 70A MOSFETs. It has a multilayered extended heatsink and 1.5mm thick LAIRD 5W/mK thermal conductivity pads to help keep temperatures manageable – which is particularly important in SFF builds.

It can hold 64 GB in two RAM slots, two PCIe 4.0 SSDs in two M.2 sockets (heatsinked and fanned), and four SATA 6 GB/s SSDs. The audio codec is the standard high-quality Realtek ALC 1220-VB, and the motherboard comes with plenty of fan headers and USB connectivity.

You can currently find this mITX motherboard for as low as $207.61 – an excellent price for a small motherboard, with huge potential.
