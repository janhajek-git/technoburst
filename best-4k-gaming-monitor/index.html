The Best 4K Gaming Monitor In 2021

If you’ve wanted to improve your gaming experience, there has never been a better time to buy a 4K gaming monitor. With recent technological developments, your options are limitless, and there is a 4K monitor for everyone.

A 4K gaming monitor will offer the best user experience, high resolution, large screen size, and fluid usability. Your games will undoubtedly be sharp and realistic.

But how do you choose the best 4K gaming monitor? What are the important things you should keep in mind, and what are the best options available in the market? 

You’ve come to the right place! Get ready to learn everything you need to know before choosing the best 4K monitor.

What Are The Benefits Of A 4K Gaming Monitor?

If you are a gamer who enjoys flawless visuals, a 4K gaming monitor is your answer. There are several benefits to choosing a 4K monitor over a traditional full HD screen.

Graphical Benefits

4K gaming monitors consist of tightly packed pixels right next to one another. Moreover, 4K resolution monitors comprise 4 times more pixels than a normal full HD screen. Due to the higher number of pixels, your gaming experience will be much sharper than before. 

Smaller details like clothing and facial expressions will be visible, and even differences in texture are noticeable. 

Wide View

The best 4K gaming monitors provide a large screen surface area. Compared to a traditional full HD screen, you can see more in-game objects in the corners and the sides in a 4K gaming monitor.

A wider field of view also makes your gaming experience realistic and intense since the screen is in your direct line of vision. 

Suitable for Consoles

4K gaming monitors are suitable for all gamers, whether you prefer the PC or console systems such as the PlayStation or Xbox.

A few consoles, such as the PlayStation 4 Pro, have been developed specifically so that they can display your games in 4K. The Xbox One S also upscales a full HD image to a 4K resolution.

Prerequisites To Using A 4K Gaming Monitor

While buying a 4K gaming monitor is bound to elevate your gaming experience, there are certain prerequisites that you will have to keep in mind:

Video Card of Your PC or Laptop

Your laptop or PC must support a 4K image signal if you want to make the most of your 4K gaming monitor. Ensure that you double-check the video card that your computer has before you buy the gaming monitor.

Gaming on a 4K monitor will require the right cable and a strong and reliable video card. Here are a few video cards (graphics) that you may consider:

  • Intel Iris Plus Graphics
  • NVIDIA Quadro Series
  • Intel UHG Graphics (from the eighth-generation Intel processors)
  • AMD Radeon RX and Pro series

Connectors and Cables

For the complete 4K monitor gaming experience, you will need an HDMI, DisplayPort, USB-C, or Thunderbolt 3 connector.

VGA and DVI connectors are older variants and will not support 4K gaming monitors. HDMI 1.4 may also be sufficient but transfers images at 30Hz, making fast-moving images appear ragged and slow.

Ensure that you choose the right cable for your connector. For the best gaming experience, the cable and connector should match perfectly. For example, a Thunderbolt 3 connector with a Thunderbolt 3 cable. Signals transfer the fastest when the cable and connector match.

Things To Look For In The Best 4K Gaming Monitor

Buying a 4K gaming monitor may seem like an easy feat, but there are multiple factors to consider. Since this is a huge investment, you cannot make this decision lightly.

If you are unaware of what to look for, the guide is here to help you. Below are some essential factors that should be present in the best 4K monitor.

Monitor Size

You are buying a gaming monitor because you want to get a complete gaming experience. That is why the size of a gaming monitor becomes a very crucial factor. If you choose small sizes, you would not be able to enjoy the gaming experience.

Ideally, the gaming monitor size should not be less than 24 inches. The bigger you go, the better your experience. However, it would help if you also remembered that as the size increases, so will the price.

Refresh Rate

Refresh rate decides the quality of your visual output and the number of times that the monitor will refresh a visual in one second. Most gaming monitors come in 120Hz or 144Hz since the frame rate is high without any breakage or stuttering.

When you choose monitors with these refresh rates, you need to ensure that the GPU can support the high frame rate. (https://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/products/docs/processors/what-is-a-gpu.html)

Some monitors come with higher refresh rates, like 165Hz or even 240Hz. As the refresh rate increases, you need to be cautious that you go for a higher GPU.

Panel Type

Monitors come in two-panel types: IPS (in-plane switching) and TN (twisted nematic). 

IPS panels are well-known for their visual quality. The picture will be more accurate in color presentation and sharpness. However, the response time is more which is not good for high-end multiplayer games.

On the other hand, the TN panel has a response time of 1ms, which is perfect for competitive gaming. Monitors having TN panels are also a more affordable choice. However, the color saturation is not great, and this could be a problem for AAA single-player games.

Response Time

Response time is then taken by a single pixel to change from black to white or other shades of grey. This is measures in milliseconds or ms.

When you buy gaming monitors, it is better to choose a higher response time since it will eradicate motion blur and ghosting (https://www.onecomputerguy.com/1ms-vs-5ms). Response time between 1ms and 4ms would be good enough for single-player games.

If you are more into playing multiplayer games, it is advisable to choose a lower response time. It would probably be better if you opt for 1ms since this would ensure no pixel response delays.

Color Accuracy

A 4K gaming monitor’s color accuracy looks into the system’s ability to provide the necessary hue level without making any rough calculations.  

A 4K gaming monitor needs to have color accuracy on the higher end of the spectrum. Most monitors follow a standard RGB pattern to enable color adjustments. But these days, sRGB is quickly becoming the best way to ensure full coverage with perfect color delivery.

The best 4K gaming monitors provide a wide color gamut based on sRGB patterns of color delivery. If the color deviates, the system will present you with an error message represented as a Delta E figure (https://www.viewsonic.com/library/creative-work/what-is-delta-e-and-why-is-it-important-for-color-accuracy/). Most experts generally consider a Delta E figure of 1.0 to be the best.

Connectors

A gaming monitor will have ports for input and output. You should try to ensure that the monitor has these connectors – DisplayPort 1.4, HDMI 1.4/2.0, or 3.5mm audio out. 

Some brands offer you other types of connectors in their monitors. However, these are the ports or connectors that are the most important. If you need to plug USB devices directly into the monitor, check for USB ports to help you do so.

Best 4K Gaming Monitor for HDR: Asus ROG Swift PG27UQ

The Asus ROG Swift PG27UQ provides immersive lighting due to its Aura Sync technology. It also supports synchronization with Aura-enabled components. 

For perfect synchronization, connect your ROG Swift PG27UQ to other peripherals after installing the Aura Sync software on your desktop or laptop. This will provide a stylish backdrop to your game and also syncs the lighting with the music or gaming effects. 

It doesn’t get better than the 144Hz refresh rate, and this 4K gaming monitor will guarantee the smoothest gameplay. Combined with a 27-inch IPS display and a DCI-P3 color gamut, your gaming experience will be realistic with lifelike contrast.

An interesting feature of the Asus ROG Swift PG27UQ is the built-in light sensor. Your screen will automatically adjust its brightness settings according to your environmental conditions. 

The ROG Swift PG27UQ also comes with ultra-low blue light technology. This will ensure maximum protection for your eyes, no matter how long you are in front of the screen. All you, as the gamer, have to do is choose from four filter settings available to you via the on-screen menu. 

However, the ROG Swift PG27UQ does not have Dolby Vision or motion blur reduction. This may hinder your gaming experience.
