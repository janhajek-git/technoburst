The Ryzen 5000 series CPUs have been announced by AMD, and PC builders are excited to get their hands on these new and improved Zen3 processors. Not only have these CPUs been optimized to play games at the maximum potential that a CPU can (currently) process, but they have also heavily increased single-core & single-thread performance, favoring workstation applications with 3D modelings, like CAD.

The best the Zen3 lineup has to offer, not just for gaming but also for content creation, is AMD new flagship Ryzen 9 5950X. This CPU fully utilizes the maximum potential of the new Zen3 architecture, as it has two CCXs (Core Complexes) in the CCD (Core Chiplet Die) that are both filled to the max with regards to cores and level 3 cache: Eight cores and 32 MB of L3 cache each, for a total of 16 cores, 32 threads, and a massive 64 MB L3 cache.

Of course, if you are in the market for the most powerful CPU available, then you’ll want to pair it with other equivalently powerful hardware components. Like a well-built chain, all of the links must fit together perfectly, because a weak piece of hardware will disrupt the functional harmony of the components, causing bottlenecking issues that will impact performance.

One of the most important modules to be paired with the CPU is the motherboard. The motherboard not only supplies power to the CPU, but it also plays a big role in regards to maintaining optimal CPU temperatures. Additionally, it allows for high-speed RAM utilization, storage capacity, cooling component connectivity, and audio. Because the Ryzen 9 5950X is the most powerful consumer market CPU available, the only motherboards worthy to be paired with it are the flagship X570 motherboards; the best that the AM4 chipset has to offer.

Best Budget Flagship Motherboard for Ryzen 9 5950X – ASRock Phantom Gaming X

If you’ve planned your budget solely around the CPU and GPU, then chances are you are going to want your other components to be as cheap as possible, but without compromising the overall build. For that, we have ASRock’s Phantom Gaming X.

This flagship motherboard comes equipped with a 14-phase Intersil ISL69147 VRM (7 true phases: 6+6, 1+1), with 50-amp power stages, 60-amp chokes, and Nichicon FP capacitors. It has an excellent thermal solution, as all of the MOSFETs are heatsinked, and the heatsinks are linked together via a heatpipe. A PCH fan is also included.

Like all flagship motherboards, the Phantom Gaming X has four RAM slots, holding up to a total of 128 GB of DDR4 RAM with supported speeds of up to 4,666 MHz. In terms of ROM, it has eight SATA III ports and three M.2 slots – all of which include a heatsink cover.

A Wi-Fi 6 module is included with this motherboard, together with eight USB slots in the rear I/O port, as well as six four-pin fans or pumps.

The audio codec included is the standard high-end Realtek ALC1220.

Best Gaming Flagship Motherboard for Ryzen 9 5950X – ASUS ROG Crosshair VIII Formula

Though the category we’ve placed this motherboard in is gaming, the ASUS ROG Crosshair VIII Formula can really work for perfectly for any type of setup.

The ASUS X570 flagship motherboard features a 16-phase (8 true, 7×2 +1×2) VRM solution, using Infineon IR3555 60-amp power stages, and a unique cooling solution. All of the MOSFETs are heatsinked, and the heatsink used is the CrossChill EK III, which makes users of copper cooling channels and EKWB (Edvard König Water Blocks) compatibility – meaning the MOSFETs can be water-cooled for temperatures up to 29.3 degrees Celsius lower than other models. A fan on the PCH is also included.

Supported RAM speeds can go up to 4,600 MHz, and for storage we have eight SATA III ports, as well as two M.2 slots – both of which include a heatsink.

Once again Wi-Fi 6 AX200 is also supported for the Crosshair VIII Formula, and the I/O panel features 12 USB slots.

Finally, the audio codec here is the Supreme FX S1220, plus an ESS Sabre ES9023P (DAC) digital to analog converter.

Despite its initial $699 MSRP, the ASUS ROG X570 Crosshair VIII Formula can currently be found for only $584.20 – an excellent price for exceptional performance.

Best Workstation Flagship Motherboard for Ryzen 9 5950X – MSI MEG X570 GODLIKE

Despite its name and RGB accents, the MSI Meg X570 Godlike is the perfect flagship motherboard for workstations, due to its massive storage capacities.

The MEG Godlike comes with an 18 phase (9 true, 7+7, 1+3) VRM solution, using Infineon TDA21472 70-amp power stages. All MOSFETs are heatsinked, with a heatpipe running through, and a fan on the PCH.

The RAM speeds that this motherboard can support are up to 4,600 MHz, and it has four PCIe 4.0 x16/-/x4 lanes. Not only can the Meg Godlike support multi-GPU setups, but it can also support six SATA III ports and five M.2 form factor SSDs – two of which through use of the bundled XPANDER-Z card, plugged in the PCIe 4.0 x16 slot. All five of these M.2 storage drives are heatsinked, and the two in the XPANDER-Z card also include a fan.

A Wi-Fi 6 AX1650 module allows for high-end Wi-Fi compatibility, and the back I/O allows for six USB drives.

The audio codec included is the Realtek ALC 1220, plus an ESS Sabre ES9018.

The MSI Meg Godlike retails for $699, though it can currently be found at a discount for $635.85.

Best Overall Flagship Motherboard for Ryzen 9 5950X – GIGABYTE X570 AORUS Xtreme

Last, but definitely not least, we have the best all-around flagship motherboard for the Ryzen 9 5950X: The Gigabyte X570 AORUS Xtreme.

This motherboard has the best VRM solution of all other flagship models, as it has an all-true 16-phase (14+2) VRM, with Infineon TDA21472 70-Amp power stages. All MOSFETs are heatsinked and a heatpipe is included.

In terms of memory and storage we have supported speeds of up to 4,400 MHz, six SATA III ports, and three M.2 slots (all heatsinked.)

Wi-Fi 6 AX200 is also included, while the back I/O can hold up to twelve USB devices.

The Audio Codec is the standard Realtek ALC1220 (VB), with an additional ESS Sabre ES9218P (DAC) converter.

The GIGABYTE X570 AORUS XTREME (Rev. 1.1) can currently be found at its MSRP $699 price tag, and it is definitely worth the cost.
