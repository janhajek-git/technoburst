NVIDIA’s GTX 1070 and GTX 1070 Ti are both graphics cards that are able to provide fantastic performance when compared to older generation graphics cards. The main difference between these two graphics cards is the performance that they can offer by the total number of CUDA cores.

NVIDIA’s GTX 1070 graphics card has originally 1,920 CUDA cores while the Ti version of the GTX 1070 has an increased number of CUDA cores, these CUDA core count has been increased to 2,432. This increase in CUDA cores allows supports higher refresh rates or larger resolutions when compared to the GTX 1070 graphics card.

The GTX 1070 Ti also offers a higher base clock speed being 1,607 MHz instead of the 1,506 MHz found on the standard GTX 1070 graphics card.

Pairing these graphics cards with a monitor is easy but depends heavily on what the user plans to use the computer for. For FPS gaming, Users having a higher overall refresh rate while taking a significant drop in the overall refresh rate. For Video editors or Photo editors, having a higher resolution instead of having a higher refresh rate is a good choice.

Budget Option – HP VH240a Monitor

For someone looking for the best budget option monitor to pair with the GTX 1070 & 1070 Ti graphics card, the HP VH240a Monitor might be the best choice for you. The 1920 x 1080p resolution featured by this monitor is paired with the ease of plugging in a variety of devices using the included VGA, USB Type-C, an HDMI port, and even a Thunderbolt port.

This monitor does feature the slowest refresh rate of the four different monitors. The HP VH240a monitor features just 60 Hz, which is still more than good enough for gaming but isn’t the high-end monitors that some gamers may want in their PC setup.

The VESA mounting allows this monitor to easily be installed onto a monitor arm, which allows for the ease of moving the monitor to where ever you may need it to!

Coincidently, this monitor is also the cheapest of all the monitors listed here, this is partly due to the lowered refresh rate of the monitor and the simple solid black design.

The GTX 1070 and GTX 1070 Ti graphics cards are able to power this budget monitor with ease. Since this monitor only has a refresh rate of up to 60 Hz the fans shouldn’t ever substantially increase in speed when opening a game.

Best Value Choice – Sceptre 24-Inch Curved Monitor

The Sceptre 24-Inch Curved 144 Hz monitor is a significant jump in quality when compared to the first monitor on this list. This monitor may have the same 1080p resolution but the refresh rate gets more than doubled, being raised from just 60 Hz to 144 Hz. This increase allows for eSports gamers to buy this monitor for their gaming setup.

This monitor features a curvature, which delivers a revolutionary visual experience. This monitor features a 1800R screen curvature, which is partly used to reduce the eye strain that gamers can experience. This curvature also increases the immersive nature that many gamers strive to achieve during their gaming experience.

It has a refresh rate of 144 Hz, which is significantly higher compared to the previous monitor that has a refresh rate of 60 Hz only. This increase allows for a much smoother gaming experience or just a smoother experience when using the main desktop screen. The 144 Hz refresh rate can be perfectly powered by either the GTX 1070 or GTX 1070 Ti graphics card.

This simple design of this monitor also allows it to fit into either an eSports gamer setup or fit into a home office that many people have been forced to build during the current COVID-19 pandemic and the quarantine.

eSports gamers will love this monitor for more than just the increase in refresh rate but also the incredibly low price it has associated with it. On Amazon, this monitor is currently listed for just $154.97.

Best Ultra-Wide Value Monitor – Sceptre 30-Inch 21:9 Curved Monitor

For gamers that want an Ultra-wide monitor but don’t want to spend $1,000 on the monitor alone, the Sceptre 30-Inch Curved monitor is a perfect choice. This monitor offers a plethora of features that can be fully utilized by the GTX 1070 and GTX 1070 Ti, this is partly due to the slightly lowered refresh rate as this monitor has a maximum refresh rate of just 85 Hz.

While some may look at this lowered refresh rate sneer against it, the 85 Hz will provide a smoother overall experience when compared to the standard 60 Hz monitor. Gamers will see an increase in smoothness during gaming but will seem a bit choppier when compared to a 144 Hz monitor.

The 2560 x 1080p resolution is easily powered by the GTX 1070 or GTX 1070 Ti graphics cards, meaning that in games you can have a much more immersive experience when compared to other 1920 x 1080p monitors.

For connectivity, this monitor features two HDMI ports and a single DisplayPort. Both of these ports are able to offer the 85 Hz refresh rate, meaning that no matter the ports available this monitor will be able to be utilized to the maximum refresh rate.

This monitor costs just $228.87, which is another reason why this monitor is labeled as the best Ultra-wide Value monitor, Just over $200 for a 30-Inch monitor is a fantastic deal overall and the only downside is that lowered refresh rate.
