The RTX 2080 / 2080 Ti is the new standard for high-end gaming. These graphics cards are so powerful, and will surely give you incredible performance and a new level of gaming realism. If you already got one of these graphics cards, we might assume that you don’t have any plan to settle for anything less when it comes to your gaming performance. However, you might also need to get a good monitor that is capable of handling the power it’s throwing so you will get the best out of your gaming display.

In this guide, we will show you the best monitors that you can partner with your RTX 2080 / 2080 Ti. All monitors that we’ve listed in this guide contain all the features and capabilities needed to maximize the full potential of your graphics card. However, there are some things you need to consider before buying a monitor.

We have also listed the Best Monitor For RTX 2070 if you want to check it out.

Things To Consider:

Monitor Resolution And Refresh Rate

Getting a monitor will vary depending on your preferences whether you prefer performance or visuals. Monitors with 1080p resolution are still the standard option for most competitive gamers. Professional players on eSports tend to play at 1080p gaming over 4k because it’s easy to achieve higher FPS, which results in a much smoother gameplay performance. On the other side, if you prefer sharper images, 1440p or 4k monitors are the great choice for you, but it will be harder to maintain high FPS since it requires more computational power especially for some AAA games. Regarding the refresh rate, if you decide to get a standard resolution, we highly recommend you to go with a higher refresh rate monitor because you will be expecting that your graphics card will throw more FPS on it. But for higher resolution monitors, it’s a fine choice to get a refresh rate that is equivalent only to the FPS you are getting. Here are the recommended resolution/Hz pairings:

  • 1920x1080p at 240Hz
  • 2560x1440p at 165Hz
  • 3440x1440p(ultra wide) at 120Hz
  • 3840x2160p (4k) at 60Hz
