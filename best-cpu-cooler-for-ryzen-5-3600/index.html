The Ryzen 5 3600 is an amazing 65W chip that is quite a pack of power in a small thermal envelope. Moreover, it provides affordable gaming power to anyone looking for it. 

However, a common issue with Ryzen 5 3600 is it overheats fast. So, the stock cooler Wraith Stealth is not enough. 

In this article, we shall walk through some of the best CPU coolers for Ryzen 5 3600 available in the market. These aftermarket CPU coolers can help you make the best out of the processor’s performance.

Also, I can help you pick the best CPU cooler for Ryzen 5 3600 for your requirements. So, let’s get started!

A Quick Buyer’s Guide

Here are some important factors to consider while picking a cooler for Ryzen 5 3600. These factors will ensure you get the best value for your hard-earned money!

Types of Coolers

There are primarily two types of coolers that you can pick from that offer excellent cooling. Here are the primary two categories: Liquid coolers and air coolers.

Liquid Coolers

Liquid coolers or water coolers use distilled water or other liquids to perform cooling. They are generally more effective at maintaining cooler temperatures during peak performance than air coolers. However, they are more difficult to maintain than air coolers.

You can go with liquid coolers if you are a gamer or someone who uses the processors at their peak for a long time. Otherwise, it may simply not be worth the money you pay for since liquid coolers are usually more expensive.

Air Coolers

Air coolers are an excellent alternative for stock coolers. Moreover, air coolers are generally popular than liquid coolers since they are effective and inexpensive.

Further, air coolers are quite larger than water coolers, yet they are available in a wide range of sizes. They also make more noise at higher speeds than liquid or water coolers.

TDP (Thermal Design Power)

TDP is one of the most important factors to consider while investing in a CPU cooler. The TDP for Ryzen 5 3600 is 65W. It refers to the heat a CPU produces when it works at its base clock.

When you are looking for a cooler, ensure the cooler has more TDP than the processor. You can find the TDP rating on the cooler’s product listing. If you do not find it on the product listing or packaging, you can check their official website.

Speed

Speed is an important factor since it plays a vital role in cooling your CPU. The processor is safe when the cooler is propelling air through at an accelerated speed.

Hence, I recommend you choose coolers with a decent spinning speed if it is an air cooler. On the other hand, ensure the liquid component is strong if you plan to purchase a liquid cooler. It should act fast and stay durable for the most efficient temperature reduction.

Noise Levels

The level of noise a cooler produces during its operation is an important factor to acknowledge. If the noise levels of the coolers are too high, it can cause many inconveniences. 

Firstly, it can disturb you with your work. Secondly, the sound waves can interfere with other components in the CPU. Although most high-quality coolers ensure to perform in lowered noises, you may have to discard a good performer for its noise levels.

RGB

RGB is an excellent feature, yet it is only an option. If you are interested in flashy lights that make your PC look edgier.

However, make sure to prioritize necessary factors such as performance, durability, and noise levels over RGB. On the other hand, if you do not like the flashy lights or think it is unnecessary, you do not have to waste your time on this feature.

Ease of Installation

Usually, AIO (All in One) coolers are more complex to install than a standard cooler. However, the ease of installation depends on the model of the cooler and your processor. Hence, it is important to pick a cooler that is easy to install on your PC.

Water or liquid coolers are more difficult to install than air coolers. It is so because it involves an extra step of installing the radiator and fans.

Size

Air coolers are generally bulkier than water coolers. However, the entire structure stays on one area instead of liquid coolers that you have to distribute across the system. You will need space for the radiator, and it has to be in the proper orientation.

Hence, if you want something effective, you can go with air coolers in a small area. Moreover, they are available in a wide range of sizes. If you want a low-profile air cooler and have enough space for alignment, you go with liquid coolers.

Are Air Coolers Better Than Liquid Coolers?

Generally, liquid coolers are better than air coolers. However, it entirely depends on the manufacturing quality and design of the cooler. 

Usually, liquid coolers have many exclusive features that make the cooling more efficient. Yet both the type of coolers are highly effective if you implement them properly. However, entry-level liquid coolers can be substantially expensive.

Should You Replace the Liquid in Liquid Coolers Often?

No, you do not have to replace it often or at any time. It requires less interaction since the coolers have auto-renew technology. In this technology, they renew the liquid automatically using flush methods.

How to Install a CPU Cooler for Ryzen 5 3600?

It is quite easy to install a cooler for Ryzen 5 3600. I have mentioned a few pointers in a step-by-step manner below to help you with the installation.

Apply the thermal paste if it is not already there. You will have to start by unscrewing the pre-attached brackets on the motherboard.

Then, you will have to mount the cooler. You can mount it away from the RAM to have fewer RAM clearance issues.

Place the best CPU cooler for Ryzen 5 3600 you pick and screw it in a diagonal pattern. Also, apply some pressure while tightening the screws as they are spring-loaded. Yet remember to tighten it gradually without pressuring it too much.

Finally, once you ensure it is properly in place, you can connect the fan header to the motherboard’s CPU fan header. Then, plug in everything that you should.
