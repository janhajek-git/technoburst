After the recent release of the Ryzen 5000 Series Zen 3 processors, AMD has also announced their Radeon RX 6000 Series GPUs. The flagship of model of this series: The Radeon RX 6900 XT.

Benchmarks released by AMD for the Radeon RX 6900 XT, show us that this GPU excels in 1440p framerates, beating its Nvidia RTX 3000 Series competition in nine out of 10 games that AMD tested for their benchmarks, as it reaches over 120 FPS in all these titles. Thus, you can expect consistent framerates for 1440p resolutions, without needing to compromise in terms of game quality settings.

Going a step above the 1440p resolution, and into the 4K gaming territory, we see that the Radeon RX 6900 XT not only meets the 60 FPS mark, but it exceeds it by quite a margin. In fact, in the aforementioned benchmarks, it reaches – and exceeds – the 120 FPS threshold necessary for 4K 144 Hz gaming in half of the titles that were tested. If one were to slightly alter the in-game quality settings, the RX 6900 XT can surely reach the required framerate for the vast majority of AAA PC titles.

To admire the full processing power that a PC, equipped with AMD’s latest Zen 3 Ryzen 5000 Series processor and RDNA 2 Radeon RX 6900 XT graphics card, can muster, requires a monitor that also has the power to display this performance potential. Each monitor will hold distinct advantages and disadvantages, which is why the monitor that will accompany a PC build should be chosen based on each persons’ distinct gaming tastes. It is for this reason why we have listed the best monitors available in multiple different resolutions and framerates, to match any gaming preference one may have.

Best 1080p Monitor for Radeon RX 6900 XT – Alienware 25 AW2518Hf

For gamers that love playing online multiplayer, and prefer buttery smooth framerates over a crisp resolution, then a 1080p 240 Hz monitor is ideal. Not only is the display perfect for quick-paced titles like first-person shooters, but it also provides exceptionally low input lag and motion blur, which can make all the difference in a competitive environment.

The Alienware 25 (inch) AW2518Hf is the perfect buy for a high-end 1080p, multiplayer-focused, gaming monitor. It has a TN panel with an 8-bit depth – 6 bits plus Frame Rate Control (FRC) – that is capable of displaying 1.68 million colors (24 bits); featuring a display area of 90.29% and a W-LED backlight with a 400-nit brightness level. Where this monitor excels is its incredibly low input lag, as it has a G2G response time of 1 ms, an average response time of 4 ms, and a measured end-to-end lag that can be as low as 19 ms.

The Alienware 25 has remarkably stylish exterior that offers a wide variety of ergonomic flexibility: like height adjustment, landscape/portrait pivot, left/right swivel, and forward/backward tilting. Additionally, it offers an amazing level of connectivity, with one USB 3.0 upstream, three USB 3.0 downstream, one USB 3.0 charge 5V/.1.5A, two HMDI 2.0 ports, one DisplayPort 1.2 slot, and both audio-in and audio-out jacks.

The Alienware 25 can currently be found for $349.99; a small price to pay for this monitor’s style and performance.

Best 1440p Monitor for Radeon RX 6900 XT – ASUS TUF Gaming VG27AQ

If you want to combine competitive gaming with single-player triple-A titles, then 1440p 144 Hz is the way to go. Not only is this resolution-framerate combo efficient, but it is also sustainable for the Radeon RX 6900 XT; as the next generation of games progresses games will become even more hardware demanding.

The monitor for the task: The ASUS TUF Gaming VG27AQ. This 27-inch, 165 Hz, monitor has an AHVA IPS panel, with an 8-bit depth, capable of displaying 1.68 million colors (99% of the sRGB spectrum) and a W-LED backlight with a brightness level that peaks at 350 nits. Even more impressive is its static contrast ratio of 1,200 to one. It has a Motion Picture Response Time (MPRT) of only 1 ms, a G2G response time of 4 ms, and a theoretical input lag of 5.3 ms.

Similar to the aforementioned Alienware 25, the VG27AQ offers a full array of ergonomic features, as well as Extreme Low Motion Blur (ELMB), AMD FreeSync technology, and two 2-Watt speakers.

The ASUS TUF Gaming VG27AQ can be purchased for $419.99, on sale from its $549 MSRP.

Best 4K 60 Hz Monitor for Radeon RX 6900 XT – BenQ EW3270U

Another stable and consistent framerate-resolution pairing that will never have you downscaling in-game graphics is the 4K resolution at 60 Hz. If you care little for competitive multiplayer and would rather focus on immersive single-player titles, this combination is for you.

The BenQ EW3270U is a 32-inch monitor that makes use of an MVA panel, with a 10-bit depth (8 bits + FRC), a max refresh rate of 76 Hz, and a potential to display over one billion colors (30 bits), with 100% of the sRGB spectrum and 95% of the DCI P3 color space being covered. It has an amazing static contrast of 3,000:1, a brightness of 300 nits, and a G2G response time of only 4 ms.

The BenQ EW3270U has a removable stand, is VESA compatible, and, in terms of ergonomics, can only be tilted forwards or backwards. It has an Advanced Motion Accelerator (AMA) feature, AMD FreeSync compatibility, a USB 3.0 Type-C port, and two 2-Watt speakers. You can find the BenQ EW3270U, currently, for $449.99.

Best 4K 144 Hz Monitor for Radeon RX 6900 XT – ACER Predator XB273K

Going all out with your monitor purchase also means you will be reaching a point beyond what the GPU is capable of rendering, at least for certain titles. But if 4K 144 Hz is what you prefer, combining a crisp resolution with a smooth framerate, then nothing beats ACER’s X27 series of monitors.

The Acer Predator X27 is a 27-inch monitor with an AHVA IPS panel with a bit depth of 10-bits (8 + FRC), that can display over one billion colors (30 bits – HDR 400 compatible) at a peak brightness of 1,000 nits. It has a static contrast ratio of 1000:1 and a low G2G response time of 4 ms. It also features Full-Array Local Dimming, Quantum Dot technology, and two 7-Watt speakers. Connectivity-wise, it features four USB 3.0 ports (3x downstream, 1x upstream).

The ACER Predator X27 can usually be found between the $1,299-$1,799 price range, though variants can be found for as low as $829.99, like the XB273K.
