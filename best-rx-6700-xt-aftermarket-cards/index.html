Why get an RX 6700 XT?

AMD and Nvidia have been dominating the GPU market for quite some time now. Even though Nivida has had the better market share out of the two, AMD is slowly catching up thanks to its RX 5000 series and now RX 6000 series. AMD’s previous generation of GPUs, the RX 5000 series, was a massive generational leap at the time, making them an up-to-date viable option for gaming at a time. Now the RX 6000 series has made that leap even bigger, and the additional features this generation features makes it worthwhile in years to come as well. This new set of Radeon GPUs is as twice as powerful as the last generation.

This RDNA 2 lineup from AMD features new compute units, new Infinity Cache technology, and large amounts of GDDR6 memory to accompany the gaming needs of the current generation of gaming. This set of PC GPUs share the same architecture found in PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S and promises to bring an improvement of up to 65% more performance per watt compared to the previous generation. The all-new AMD Infinity Cache that is integrated into this set of GPUs allows high bandwidth performance at low power and low latency. However, what is most eye-catching about the RX 6000 series is its choice of more VRAM than the competition. These massive high-speed memories allow these graphics cards to handle 4K gaming without any issues, even in the most demanding titles.

Out of the four cards in this lineup, RX 6700 XT is the latest card and gives solid 1440p and 1080p performance for the cash you spend. The RX 6700 XT packs 12 GB of GDDR6 VRAM that has a memory bandwidth of up to 384 GB per second to handle even the heaviest workloads without a sweat. The base frequency of this GPU is 2321 MHz and can boost up to 2581 MHz. Just like the other models of this lineup, RX 6700 XT also comes with hardware accelerated Ray Tracing technology, and you can enjoy almost any title up-to-date with the highest fidelity options in the way they are meant to be played. Unfortunately, these cards are as scarce as hens’ teeth right now, and you might not be able to find a reference card at all. But worry not; there are plenty of aftermarket RX 67000 XT cards out in the market right now, and they are often better than the original RX 6700 XT cards.

ASUS ROG Strix Radeon RX 6700 XT OC

ASUS ROG Strix has always been a leading brand in the PC component market, and its GPUs are also often praised for their amazing build quality and thermals. The ASUS ROG Strix RX 6700 XT comes with its iconic triple-fan setup alongside a thicker heatsink to provide optimal performance to any hardcore gamer. This graphics card might just be the one if you are looking for a decent card that can push the limits without thermal throttling. ASUS brings an AIB similar to their previous ROG Strix cards. These well-designed AIBs also the OC mode of them pushing the GPUs to their full potentials. This ROG Strix RX 6700 XT can achieve around 2820 MHz from its overclocking options and a little tweaking. The card also features three DisplayPort 1.4’s and one HDMI 2.1 as the interface.

While the performance and thermals seem quite promising in this card, what stands out most is its design. The ASUS ROG Strix hardware brand is extremely popular for its gamer aesthetics more than its actual value, and this RX 6700 XT is no exception. Even though the card is quite long, it has ‘gamer-ness’ written all over it with its flashy design with futuristic edges and, most importantly, RGB. The lighting of this card can be easily customized through the official ASUS RGB application, Aura, and it might really bring out a beautiful look to your PC, especially if you have got ROG Strix hardware in your build. However, as enticing as all of this sounds, the card has one major downside, and that is its pricing. You might need to spend a few extra bucks than the base price of an RX 6700 XT to get this beast.

MSI Radeon RX 6700 XT Gaming X


Gaming X is one of MSI’s best sub-brands, and its GPUs are often highly praised by consumers around the world for their durability and performance. MSI Radeon RX 6700 XT Gaming X is no exception as it brings all the goodness of Gaming X to the latest AMD GPU lineup. However, most Gaming X GPUs come with the term “Trio” at the end of their names, and this card does not. This is mainly because the Gaming X RX 6700 XT only comes with two fans instead of the famous three fan combo. But don’t you lose hope just yet because it does not fail to bring outstanding thermal performance in a good value package.

In the performance department, this card also has great headroom for overclocking. This well-made AIB can normally maintain an average boost clock of about 2680 MHz and can reach up to 2750 MHz with a little tweaking to give you quite noticeable performance gains. A triple-fan setup could have increased its boost clock speeds significantly, but it is also worth noting that this particular design consumes less power overall. The dual-fan setup of this card has also made it way shorter than most other RX 6700 XTs out there. Unfortunately, to the disappointment of gamers, the RGB light bar on the side has been removed completely, but it still has a bit of lighting left on its logo and its shroud. The card also comes with three DisplayPort 1.4’s and one HDMI 2.1 as the interface.

ASUS TUF Gaming RX 6700 XT OC

The Ultimate Force or TUF is one of ASUS’s hardware brands that prioritizes durability over the looks. Just like the motherboards and other components in this trademark, its GPUs are also made well to last longer than the average. Similarly, the ASUS TUF Gaming RX 6700 XT also highlights durability over the looks and fancy lighting with its all-aluminium body. It also features the iconic axial-tech fan design alongside a vented backplate to make sure it doesn’t face any issues in the thermals department. Even though the card itself might not look as fancy or powerful, its build quality and performance make sure that you aren’t missing out on the party.

The entire GPU has a uniform look with its dark-coloured and brushed metal finish. However, you aren’t completely missing out on RGB either. Its logo on the sidelights fulfil any RGB lover’s expectations, and the lighting effects are fully customizable alongside other RGB components of your build. With its unique triple-fan cooling setup, the card can easily achieve 2534 MHz and can even reach up to 2,622 MHz in boost mode. This card also has three DisplayPort 1.4’s and one HDMI 2.1 as the interface.

Conclusion

These are the three best RX 6700 XT aftermarket cards you can get right now. All of them have their own strengths and weaknesses so the one you should go for depends on your priorities. In the overall battle, ASUS ROG Strix Radeon RX 6700 XT OC comes out on top as it not only has a flashy gamer design, but it also manages to perform the best, giving out the highest clock speeds on this list. MSI Radeon RX 6700 XT Gaming X will be an ideal fit if you are opting for a less power-consuming and a more compact GPU to fit in your setup. And finally, if you are looking for a rugged graphics card to last sometime in the heaviest conditions, ASUS TUF is easily the one you should go for. Regardless of the brand and model you go with, RX 6700 XT is a great choice all the way, especially if you are a gamer and a content creator.
