Things To Consider:

Resolution & Refresh Rate

Getting a monitor always depends on your preferences whether you lean toward performance or visuals. Some people said that the RTX 2070 / 2070 Super is overkilled for a 1080p monitor, but for some competitive gamers, it is still the standard resolution because it’s easy to achieve higher FPS, which results in a much smoother gameplay experience. On the other side, if you prefer sharper images, we recommend you to get a 1440p or 4k monitor, but you need to take note that it will be harder to achieve higher FPS especially for some graphically demanding games. In terms of the refresh rate, we recommend you to go with a much higher refresh rate if you get a 1080p resolution because you will be expecting that your graphics card will throw more FPS on it. But for higher resolution monitors, getting a refresh rate that is equivalent to the FPS you’re getting is a fine choice for you. Here are the recommended resolution and refresh rate pairings for your RTX 2070 / 2070 Super.

  • 1920x1080p at 165Hz
  • 2560x1440p at 144Hz
  • 3440x1440p(ultra wide) at 144Hz
  • 3840x2160p (4k) at 60Hz

G-Sync Compatibility

You might wanna consider getting a monitor with G-Sync compatibility since the RTX 2070 features G-Sync technology. G-Sync monitors are becoming so popular these days because it gives a big impact on the overall gaming performance. G-sync technology resolves screen tearing issues, which results in a much more pleasurable visual experience. It might be a bit pricey to get a G-Sync monitor, but it is a good investment for futureproofing your RTX 2070 if ever you plan to upgrade your card in the future or perhaps want to run dual RTX 2070 Build.
