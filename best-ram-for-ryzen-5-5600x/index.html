Its been an exciting moment for gamers as AMD announced their Zen 3 Ryzen 5000 series CPUs, and as time goes by, we get additional tidbits of information regarding the performance of these highly anticipated processors.

The most recent release of information we’ve had dealt with the single-thread performance of the least expensive Zen 3 model: The Ryzen 5 5600X. Not only did these PassMark benchmarks show a generational leap of 47%, compared to the (Ryzen 5 3600XT) but they also display the Ryzen 5 5600X outperforming every single 9th and 10th generation Intel CPU by a margin of at least 10% – its difference with the i9-10900K. Considering how much gaming applications are dependent on single-core performance, as well as the fact that few if any, games utilize more than 6 cores and 12 threads, this CPU is primed to be an excellent money-for-value option for gaming rigs.

To maintain high framerates, the CPU depends heavily on the performance of the RAM it’s paired with. Choosing the proper speed and latency can affect performance by a huge margin – as shown by GamersNexus’s benchmarks, proving that the i5-10600K can outperform the i9-10900K solely based on RAM kits.

When purchasing RAM, there are certain aspects that are incredibly important to ensure compatibility. Such aspects include: The storage size, the overall speed of the RAM (which includes both clock speed and CAS latency), whether the RAM is a single channel or dual channel, how clunky the heat-spreader or heatsinks are (to make sure they fit underneath the CPU cooler of choice or an SFF build) and, of course, whether or not they feature RGB lighting for those that want to game in style.

Taking all the above into consideration, we’ve compiled a list of the best RAM for the Ryzen 5 5600X, depending on speed, storage, and price.

Best Budget 3600 MHz CL 16 RAM for Ryzen 5 5600X – Crucial Ballistix RGB

Crucial, a brand of Micron, makes some of the best memory units for both RAM and ROM SSDs, at incredibly low prices. Their Crucial Ballistix RGB series RAM units provide high frequencies, low CAS latency, and RGB lighting to boot – and they are also available in three colors: White, black, and red.

When it comes to RAM speeds, gaming benchmarks prove (Wolfgang, GamerNexus) that the current sweet spot lies between 3,200 MHz at CL14, and 3,600 MHz at CL16, with the slight performance edge going to the latter. Of course, the former can be overclocked to a 3,600 MHz CL15 speed, which will outperform the 3,600 MHz variants; but if you don’t want to go through the trouble of overclocking, 3,600 MHz CL16 is the way to go.

The Crucial Ballistix RGB features 16 GB of 3,600 CL16 RAM, with a low-profile form factor and aluminum heat spreaders. The RGB atop the RAM includes 16 LEDs separated into eight zones, and is compatible with ASUS Aura, MSI Mystic Light, Gigabyte AORUS, and more. XMP 2.0 profiles are also included, for automatic overclocking.

Considering that these RAM two ranks of 8 GB cost only $89.99, buying the Ballistix RGB is a no-brainer for gaming enthusiasts.

Best Performance 3600 MHz CL16 RAM for Ryzen 5 5600X – G.Skill Trident Z Neo Series

For those looking to invest a bit extra for high-quality Samsung B-die RAM, G.Skill offers their stylish Trident Z Neo Series RAM units.

Once again clocking at 3,600 MHz, with a 16-19-19-39 (CAS 16) timing and a Samsung B-die (which is optimized for AMD Ryzen Series CPUs) the Trident Neo Z Series offers incredible performance with a flashy exterior: A dual-tone, brushed aluminum heat spreader is enhanced with a tri-finned, beveled, edge and an 8-zone RGB lighting running across the top.

Each rank of RAM also includes a 10-layer PCB and hand screened memory integrated circuits (ICs), for better durability and reliability.

Two ranks of 8 GBs each, of the G.Skill Trident Z Neo Series, will only cost you $114.99; which is a bit higher than the aforementioned Crucial Ballistix RGB, but arguably a small price to pay for the integrated Samsung B die which works best with Ryzen series CPUs.

Best Budget 3200 MHz CL14 RAM for Ryzen 5 5600X – G.SKILL Flare X Series

RAM units with lesser clock speeds and lower CAS latency, will perform similarly – and, in some cases, even better – than higher speed and latency models. The 3,200 MHz CL14 RAM provides you with the flexibility to overclock to 3,600 or 3,800 MHz, at CL15 or CL16, so if you are willing to overclock your RAM, they will provide you with a significant performance boost.

G.Skill, this time with the Flare X Series model, offers a 3,200 MHz CL14 model at an incredibly low price; keeping all the same performance-dependent hardware but removing any RGB flare in order to focus on function rather than style. Note that these units are not low-profile, so chances are they will not fit properly into a small form factor (SFF) case.

This 3,200 MHz, 14-14-14-34 CAS timings, a dual-channel kit of 16 GB (8 GB per rank) comprise the hardware of the Flare X Series, with additional XMP 2.0 compatibility that allows for automatic overclocking via the use of preset profiles.

Considering that this 16 GB of RAM costs only $99.99, makes them the best price-for-value RAM available for the specification parameters.

Best Performance 3200 MHz CL14 RAM for Ryzen 5 5600X – TEAMGROUP T-Force Dark Pro

Whether it be for workstations or gaming rigs, the TeamGroup T-Force Dark Pro RAM is the best 3,200 MHz CL 14 (14-14-14-31) RAM that money can buy. It can transfer data at speeds of up to 24.6 GB/s, making use of a JEDEC RC 2.0 custom circuit board that separates power and signal layers, increasing transmission signal by up to 35%.

Also making use of XMP technology, the T-Force Dark Pro allows for multiple overclocking profiles that can be customized for one-step overclocking. What’s particularly special about this RAM, is the fact that it has a five-layer heatsink, in addition to its aluminum heat spreader, which allows for stable overclocking without the worry of overheating the RAM.

Given the speed, the quality of build, and the integrated Samsung B die, the $129.99 price tag of the TeamGroup T-Force Dark Pro is incredibly low, even considering its lack of RGB lighting.
