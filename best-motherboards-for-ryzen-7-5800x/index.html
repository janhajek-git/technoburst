AMD has recently announced their new Zen 3 processors, and the mid-range Ryzen 7 5800X is looking to be a gaming powerhouse.

This CPU makes full use of a single core complex (CCX) on its core chiplet die (CCD), which contains all eight of its cores, 16 threads, and its full 32 MB of level 3 cache. Because of this enhanced core layout, found exclusively in the Zen 3 series CPUs – with the only exception being the Ryzen 3 3300X, which had four cores and eight threads in a single-CCX layout – the internal latencies have been reduced dramatically, to the point where these AMD’s processors can now claim Intel’s crown as the best CPU manufacturer for gaming applications as their own.

The reason why the Ryzen 7 5800X appears to be the ultimate CPU for gaming – even more so than the Ryzen 9 processors – is because current, and even next-generation, games will not use more than eight cores and 16 threads; as these are the limitations of the Xbox Series X and Playstation 5. Therefore, having more than cores than what can fit in a single CCX will become redundant, since the internal communication will have to pass between CCXs (through AMD’s infinity fabric) which will have the same internal latency found in Zen 2 processors.

 To optimize the use of the Ryzen 7 5800X, and maximize its overclocking capabilities, a motherboard of equal caliber must be paired with it. One that has solid VRM and thermal solutions that will supply the CPU with sufficient power without causing it to overheat. One that supports high-speed RAM that compliments the performance of the CPU, one that allows for an ample amount of SSD storage, a respectable audio codec, and the connectivity necessary to accommodate case fans and external USB devices.

To assist in your search for finding the perfect motherboard that pairs with the Ryzen 7 5800X, we’ve compiled a list of the best motherboards based on price, performance, and form factor.

Best Budget Motherboard for Ryzen 7 5800X – ASUS Prime X570-P

Spent too much money on the CPU and GPU, and need a respectable motherboard that won’t break the bank? We fully understand; and hence why we recommend the ASUS Prime X570-P.

With a VRM solution that features twelve 50-amp Vishay power stages (4×2, 2×2 layout), this motherboard offers power without falling short on thermals. All MOSFETs on this motherboard are heatsinked, and a fan is included on the PCH.

The Prime X570-P offers plenty of RAM storage capacity as well, with 128 GB of DDR4 memory, with speeds of up to 4,400 MHz – though 3,200 to 3,600 MHz, at CL14 and CL16 respectively, should suffice.

The ASUS Prime X570-P offers an amazing amount of connectivity for its price tag. Two PCIe 4.0 M.2 slots, six SATA III ports, four USB 3.1 Type-A, two USB 3.0 Type-A, and two USB 2.0 ports.

The only downside of this motherboard is its audio codec, as it utilizes the Realtek S1200A: Decent, but not great.

Considering the price of the ASUS Prime X570-P – which retails for $159.99, but can currently be found for $139.13 – it offers power, versatility, and value at a fraction of the cost of its competitors.

Best Gaming Motherboard for Ryzen 7 5800X – ASUS ROG Crosshair VIII Hero

Optimizing your setup with the best hardware available for gaming, means you are going to have to spend a bit extra to get the best components money can buy. The motherboard that fits these criteria, is the ASUS ROG Crosshair VIII Hero.

The Crosshair VIII Hero offers a high-end 14+2 phase setup and 60-amp power stages – all of which are heatsinked and include top of the line embedded heatpipes to dissipate heat and keep temperatures low. Having both power and low thermals, means that you can safely, and reliably, overclock your Ryzen 7 5800X to your heart’s content.

When it comes to RAM, the Crosshair VIII Hero can accommodate up to 126 GB of DDR4 memory, with speeds reaching 4,600 MHz.

Connectivity wise, we have two PCIe 4.0 compatible M.2 slots – both of which are heatsinked – as well as eight SATA III ports. Additionally, eight USB 3.2 Gen 2 slots (one Type-C, the others Type-A) and four USB 3.2 Gen 1 ports (all Type-A) can be found on the back I/O port.

The ROG Crosshair VIII Hero features a SupremeFX S1220 codec, probably the best audio codec available in the motherboard market. It has a 120 dB SNR stereo playback and 113 dB SNR recording input; as well as Sonic Studio III, Sonic Radar III, DTS and Sound Unbound which are also included.

A bit expensive at $359.99, but still worth every penny.

Best Hybrid Motherboard for Ryzen 7 5800X – MSI Meg X570 Unify

The Ryzen 7 5800X has the power not just for gaming, but also for mid-end content creation utility. If you plan on using your PC for both types of applications, the MSI Meg X570 Unify is an excellent choice.

With 12+2+1 phases, 60-amp power stages, and 12 titanium chokes, the VRM design of this motherboard is ideal for stable overclocking, while the large extended heatsinks, server-grade PCB, and its built-in PCH fan allow for worry-free prolonged use.

The Meg Unify can support 128 GB of RAM separated in four slots, with speeds that reach up to an, almost excessive, 5,000 MHz frequency.

In terms of storage, we have three M.2 slots (all three of which are heatsinked and support PCIe 4.0), four SATA III ports, and the back I/O includes eight USB ports (1x Gen2 Type-C, 3x Gen 2 Type-A, 2x Gen1 Type-A, 2x 2.0).

The audio codec included is the, standard for high-end motherboards, Realtek ALC 1220.

You can currently find the MSI Meg X570 Unify for only $299.99.

Best ITX Motherboard for Ryzen 7 5800X – Gigabyte X570I AORUS Pro Wi-Fi

SFF builds are constantly gaining popularity, and the R7 5800X is a great choice for such setups. Hence why we’ve included the best X570 ITX motherboard in the market: The Gigabyte AORUS Pro Wi-Fi.

Despite its smaller size, this motherboard features an all-true, eight-phase, VRM solution with 40A power stages and 70A MOSFETs. It has plenty of thermal optimization hardware, including a multilayered extended heatsink, 1.5mm thick LAIRD thermal pads, and a PCH fan.

Two memory slots hold a max of 64 GB of DDR4 RAM, with supported speeds of up to 4,400 MHz.

For an ITX motherboard, the fact that the AORUS Pro Wi-Fi has two M.2 slots is quite incredible. Also, four SATA III ports are available for additional SSD storage units.

The back I/O makes use of six USB slots: One USB 3.1 Type-C, one USB 3.1 Type-A, and four USB 3.0, also Type-A.

The audio codec is, once again, the high-end standard, Realtek ALC 1220-VB.

For a small, yet powerful, ITX motherboard, look no further than the AORUS Pro Wi-Fi, which you can currently find for $212.99.
